Pentathlon of the Muses

From 1912 to 1948, artists could win Olympic medals in five major events. More than 2,000 tried. Only 45 took gold.

The Olympic Art Competitions were official events at the Olympic Games from 1912 to 1948. Known to their founder as the Pentathlon of the Muses, these contests awarded medals in architecture, music, literature, painting, and sculpture for works about sport. Over seven Olympiads in seven cities, more than 2,000 artists participated from around the world.

The Olympic ArtMap allows us to visualize that participation. Using data from over 3,000 submissions, this interactive map tracks Olympic artworks' origins, destinations, and success. What we discover are not just the statistics of a single competition, but global trends illustrating one of the most complex periods in art history--the decades of modernism--in new and surprising ways.
